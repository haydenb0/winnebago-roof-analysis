Background & Dispute History

This coach has a fiberglass roof that began showing hairline cracks along the radius edge in 2021. I contacted Winnebago at that time and have been engaged with their customer care team ever since. The key milestones:

October 2021
First contacted Winnebago about hairline cracks along the roof radius. Barber RV (Ventura, CA) evaluated the roof and submitted findings directly to Winnebago's tech team.
Early 2022
Winnebago facilitated a spray-on bed liner coating repair at Barber RV to address the cracking. The repair did not address the root cause of delamination.
January 2022
Bubbles forming; fiberglass skin beginning to visibly separate from the underlayment. Reported to Winnebago.
December 2023
Documented progressive delamination using a spirit level on the roof — measurable unevenness confirmed across the roof surface. Delamination and ridge formation continuing to worsen.
March 18, 2025
Winnebago offered in writing: "we are willing to offer a goodwill replacement of the roof — with the stipulation being that the issue is not determined to be due to lack of sealant maintenance." One condition. No mention of parts-only or labor caps.
March 20, 2025
When I called to schedule, Winnebago walked back the offer to "parts only." This was a unilateral change after I had accepted the terms.
April 17, 2025
After requesting full labor coverage, Winnebago countered: factory estimates 104 hours total. They will cap my labor responsibility at 80 hours at $209/hr — meaning I pay $16,720 in labor; they contribute 24 hours ($5,016). Escalation to management held at this offer.
Offer expires: August 31, 2026
The Winnebago offer remains open at the parts + 80-hour labor cap terms through August 31, 2026.
June 2026
Active moisture intrusion discovered at two ceiling light fixtures near the front AC unit. Roof inspection found stress fractures in the filon directly above the moisture points. No sealant failures found. Obtained independent estimate from Accent RV Roofing (Corona, CA).

Roof System: OEM Fiberglass / Filon

Material Characteristics

MaterialFiberglass-reinforced plastic (filon) panel, bonded to luan plywood substrate with contact adhesive
ConstructionRigid laminated panel system — filon skin adhered to foam/plywood composite using contact cement
Failure modeAdhesive bond degrades over time under thermal cycling and UV exposure; skin separates from substrate (delamination). Seams and penetrations require periodic re-caulking.
MaintenanceAnnual inspection and re-caulking of seams, radius edges, and all penetration points required
Industry notesAccent RV notes this design "often fails due to perimeter separation, lack of mechanical fasteners at roof radius, fiberglass cracking caused by flexing and thermal expansion, and aging adhesives that lose bond strength." This is the same design that has been failing on this coach since 2021.

Roof System: ARV RoofTech™

ARV RoofTech™ is Accent RV's proprietary multi-layer membrane roofing system. It is not an OEM fiberglass replacement — it replaces the filon panel technology entirely with a bonded elastomeric membrane system using the same material technology found in commercial flat roofing, bridge waterproofing, and industrial applications. Full technical details: rvroofoc.com/arv-roof-tech.

Layer 1: High-Bond Acrylic Base Membrane

Material classWater-based elastomeric acrylic coating
FunctionCreates the adhesion foundation; bonds to the fresh luan plywood substrate; primes the surface for reinforcement fabric
Other applicationsWidely used on commercial flat roofs (warehouses, schools, hospitals), metal roofs, modified bitumen systems. Standard commercial roofing technology with decades of track record.

Layer 2: Poly-Flex™ Reinforcement Fabric

Material classScrim-reinforced polyester fabric (proprietary blend — Accent RV's formulation)
FunctionEmbedded into wet base layer; adds dimensional stability and tear resistance; bridges stress points and seams; prevents cracking at penetrations and perimeter edges
Key benefitEliminates the need for caulking at seams — seams become part of the reinforced membrane, not a separate sealant bead that shrinks and cracks over time
Other applicationsScrim-reinforced membranes are used in bridge deck waterproofing, tunnel liners, foundation waterproofing, geomembranes (pond and landfill liners), and industrial flooring

Layer 3: Polyurethane-Modified Elastomeric Topcoat

Material classPolyurethane-modified elastomeric coating (two full spray coats + final topcoat)
FunctionFinal weatherproof shell; handles UV, heat, vibration, ponding water, and foot traffic
UV performanceFormulated for high solar reflectivity and long-term UV stability. Bright white reflective finish reduces interior temperatures. Accent RV's shop is in the Inland Empire — UV and desert heat performance is tested locally.
FlexibilityHigh elongation — flexes with road vibration and thermal expansion rather than cracking. This is the inverse of why filon fails: filon is rigid, this is flexible.
Other applicationsCommercial flat roofing, spray foam roof systems, marine deck coatings, truck bed liners (Rhino Liner / Line-X chemistry), parking garage waterproofing, bridge deck protection, stadium surfaces

Adhesive: Stabond Commercial Contact Adhesive

ManufacturerStabond Corporation — commercial adhesives manufacturer
Other applicationsRV and composite vehicle manufacturing; aerospace and defense (fiberglass composites, aluminum bonding); synthetic turf installation. High-temperature resistance formulations.

Installation Process

All ARV RoofTech™ installations are performed indoors in a climate-controlled environment to eliminate moisture contamination, dew, and temperature inconsistency during curing — ensuring maximum adhesion and membrane integrity.

  1. Tear-off and inspection: Remove all roof parts, existing membrane, sealants, and adhesive. Inspect plywood substrate for water damage or rot. Repair any structural issues found.
  2. New substrate: Install fresh luan plywood (9 sheets, 4'×8'×¼") over the full roof for a clean bonding surface.
  3. Base membrane + fabric: Apply acrylic base while wet; embed Poly-Flex™ fabric; apply second membrane coat over fabric to fully encapsulate and bond the reinforcement layer.
  4. Seam and penetration reinforcement: Using same base + fabric, seal all perimeter edges, both side rails, front and rear cap transitions, and every roof penetration (AC units, vents, antennas, skylight). These areas become part of the unified membrane — no caulk beads.
  5. Topcoat: Two spray coats of elastomeric base followed by polyurethane-modified topcoat across entire roof surface.
  6. Aluminum radius and trim: Install aluminum radius moldings on both sides; install flat trim moldings. Color-tinted to blend with RV exterior.
